Video Guessing Game

Welcome to the Video Guessing Experiment by the Adcock Lab at Duke University!

Please carefully review the instructions below and on the following page about how to navigate the task. These will not be repeated and you will not be able to return to the instructions once you start the task.

In each video you watch, there will be randomly placed pauses, between 1 - 3 per video, where we will ask you to answer a few questions about the video (e.g. How much do you want to see the rest of the drawing? or How confident are you about what the drawing is going to be?).

We will also ask you to make a guess about what you think the drawing will be. We expect your guess might evolve or change over the video and we are interested in what that process looks like. If you do not have a guess to make, simply write "idk" (I don't know) or "none".

Try to use the most common English words for your guesses. For example, if you see an image of a hamster write "hamster" instead of "animal" or "Winter White Dwarf hamster".

At the end of each pause, you get to decide whether you would like to continue watching the current video ("Continue Watching") or move on to the next video ("Advance to Next") by selecting the appropriate button. If you elect to move on to the next video, you will not be able to see the final drawing.

When watching these videos please try your best to focus your attention on the line to give us the best understanding of how and when you can identify what it being drawn.

To continue playing the video after it's been paused or to advance to the next video, you must answer all questions and then press the Continue Watching Button to continue watching or press the Advance to Next Button to bring up the post-video questions. You will be prompted to answer all the questions if you happen to forget one. You must move all sliders on the screen (if your answer is at the mid-point of a slider you must move it off and back into the middle).

If you choose to advance to the next video, you will first be brought to the questions you would answer at the end of each video. After you answer these questions, you can move on to the next video.

At the end of a trial, you will have the option to rewatch the video or move to the next video. To select either option, you must first type in your final guess and again, move ALL slider scales. You, again, will be prompted to answer all of the questions if you happen to forget one.

After these things are done the "Next Video" button will take you to the next video and the "Re-watch" button will play the video again, this time with no interruptions.

You will receive alerts that you are halfway through the task and when you have 5 videos left to go. Again, we anticipate the task taking about 25-30 minutes to complete.

At the end of the 25 pre-selected videos, you will have the option to continue watching more videos if you want to. These additional videos will function just like the previous ones except now you will have the option to terminate the experiment either during the video (at pause points) or after the video has finished. At this point, you may watch as many or as few of the videos as you want to. These videos are a bonus and do not impact your compensation.

Depending on you browser and internet connection, sometimes a video will freeze for a handful of seconds. If this happens, please DO NOT try to hit the Refresh button or move backwards in the task.

Videos will start playing automatically when you hit the button to advance (including the "Start" button below).

Press start to begin the task.
